Co-operative Education

I also specialize in Digital Arts Communication, a program which focuses on the digital design of hypertext, image, sound, and video. The co-op program allows me to alternate between work terms and school study terms. This lets me try a variety of different jobs at different companies while still studying for my undergraduate degree.

Work Experience

I've worked in a variety of different places, in many different roles. My first job was as a graphic artist at The Artona Group, the largest graduation photography company in Vancouver. This was my first taste of what the graphic design's industry as well as my first time in an actual working environment. It was very fast-paced, and I was able to be very creative in that job.

I've also worked in retail as a salesperson. I developed my people skills and my selling skills. It was a highly stressful environment as I worked during a huge sale period. From this I learned that the business environment is a highly stressful one, and in particular, retail.

One of my more recent jobs was working as a receptionist for a large equity loan corporation. I was responsible for ordering supplies, redirecting phone calls to brokers, and data entry. In this job, I had to be very careful with all my work because I was working with money and had to be very precise with my data entry. This was also the first time that I was the front line of customer interaction, on the phone lines, as well as in person. I learned how to be professional and present myself in a business environment.

Each of these places had a different environment, and I learned how to adapt and grow to each situation. As an entrepreneur I must know how to adapt and change the way I act in a business setting, particularly when speaking clients or investors.
