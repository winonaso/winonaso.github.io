Luddite Lights

Coursework

In my upper year design course, we had to emulate a course reading by hacking a DIY project and an arduino. The design project started with choosing a course reading we wanted to cover - in our case, it was Steven Jones' Against Technology.

We soldered buttons to wires, figured out how to attach all of the lights and buttons, attached them all to the arduino, and uploaded our code to the arduino.

We were nominated and won the University of Waterloo English Department’s 2014 Rhetoric and Digital Design Award for our work on this project.

To see our entire design process from beginning to end, check out my course blog, Our Leaderless Movement.
